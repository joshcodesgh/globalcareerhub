Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about finding remote work and using our platform.

Is Global Career Hub free for job seekers?

Yes, absolutely. Our service is 100% free for job seekers. We believe that everyone deserves equal access to career opportunities. You will never be asked to pay to view or apply for jobs.

How do you find your jobs? Are they legitimate?

We aggregate jobs from multiple trusted, high-quality sources like Remotive, Findwork, and RemoteOK, as well as directly from company career pages. We focus on sources known for legitimate remote work to minimize the risk of scams. While we do our best to vet the sources, we always recommend you research a company before applying.

How do I apply for a job?

When you find a job you're interested in, simply click the "Apply Now" button. This will take you directly to the original job posting on the employer's website or the source platform. You can then follow their specific application instructions. We are not involved in the application process itself.

Do you have jobs for people with no experience?

Yes. While many jobs require experience, we also list entry-level positions, internships, and roles that provide on-the-job training. Use keywords like "entry-level," "junior," or "trainee" in your search, and be sure to check out our Resources page for platforms that are great for beginners.

What equipment do I need to start working remotely?

At a minimum, you will need a reliable computer (laptop or desktop) and a stable internet connection. For specific roles like customer support, you may also need a noise-cancelling headset. We recommend having a dedicated workspace to stay productive.

How do I get paid by international companies?

Payment methods vary by company. Common options include direct bank transfer, PayPal, Payoneer, or Wise. In some regions, companies may use services that deposit directly to mobile money wallets. Always clarify payment methods during the interview process.

Why was my application rejected?

Remote jobs are competitive. Rejection can happen if your skills don't match the specific requirements, or if the position has already been filled. Don't be discouraged. Improve your CV, tailor your cover letter for each application, and keep applying. Check our Blog for tips on improving your applications.
